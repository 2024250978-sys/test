Sales & Service Integration: Lessons from Our Dessert Campaign

Published: January 10, 2026 | Category: Sales Strategy | Based on: MKT572 Sales Campaign

Our dessert sales campaign at Padang Mahsyar taught me that successful sales management is about creating value, not just making transactions...

The MKT572 Sales Management project gave me hands-on experience in planning and executing a real sales campaign. Setting up our dessert stall at Padang Mahsyar was challenging but incredibly educational—we not only broke even but made a profit by applying sales principles we learned in class.

Key Learnings from Our Sales Campaign:

  • Customer-Centric Approach: We discovered that customers responded better to explanations about our homemade ingredients than to aggressive sales pitches
  • Upselling Strategy: By offering dessert combos at a slightly higher price, we increased our average transaction value by 25%
  • Inventory Management: Balancing product variety with inventory control was crucial—we learned to forecast demand based on time of day and customer flow
  • Location Optimization: Choosing Padang Mahsyar parking area gave us high visibility but also taught us about timing—mornings were for breakfast desserts, afternoons for sweet treats

Connecting Sales to Service Excellence:

  • Post-Sale Service: We offered simple packaging tips for customers taking desserts home, which turned into unsolicited testimonials
  • Feedback Collection: We used sales interactions to gather feedback about flavors and presentation, showing customers we valued their opinions
  • Relationship Building: Regular customers from nearby offices began to recognize our team, creating a sense of community around our stall
  • Ethical Sales Practices: We were transparent about ingredients and pricing, which built trust and differentiated us from competitors

Application to Service Management:

This experience reinforced that sales and service are interconnected. Good sales management creates positive first impressions that set the stage for ongoing service relationships. It taught me that:

  • Every sales interaction is a service opportunity
  • Customer education during sales builds long-term loyalty
  • Ethical sales practices align with service excellence principles
  • Sales data provides insights for service improvement

Moving forward, I see sales management not as separate from service delivery but as its crucial first chapter—one that determines whether customers will return for the rest of the story.

-- Example: Create Blogs Table
CREATE TABLE blogs (
    blog_id INT PRIMARY KEY AUTO_INCREMENT,
    user_id INT,
    title VARCHAR(255),
    content TEXT,
    category_id INT,
    created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
    FOREIGN KEY (user_id) REFERENCES users(user_id),
    FOREIGN KEY (category_id) REFERENCES categories(category_id)
);

SWOT Analysis & Key Learnings

This project introduced me to SWOT analysis in system development. Here's how I applied it to evaluate the CoolBlog prototype:

Strengths
  • User-friendly interface design
  • Scalable database architecture
  • Comprehensive feature set
  • Responsive design for mobile
Weaknesses
  • Limited real-time collaboration features
  • Basic search functionality
  • No built-in analytics dashboard
  • Learning curve for advanced features
Opportunities
  • Integration with social media platforms
  • Monetization features for bloggers
  • AI-powered content suggestions
  • Mobile app development
Threats
  • Competition from established platforms
  • Data privacy regulations
  • Security vulnerabilities
  • Rapid technology changes
Key Insights from SWOT Analysis

The SWOT analysis taught me to think critically about system design from multiple perspectives. I learned that while our prototype had strong technical foundations (strengths), we needed to address user experience gaps (weaknesses) and consider market competition (threats) alongside growth potential (opportunities).

"SWOT analysis is not just a business tool—it's a critical thinking framework that helps developers create more robust, user-centered systems by anticipating challenges and leveraging opportunities."
